Denoising X-rays

A deep learning-based medical imaging system that enhances low-dose chest X-ray images by removing Poisson noise using the DnCNN architecture, improving image quality while preserving important anatomical details for better clinical diagnosis.

Project Overview

Chest X-rays are widely used for diagnosing lung diseases, but low-dose imaging often introduces Poisson noise that reduces image quality and affects diagnosis. This project proposes a DnCNN-based deep learning framework trained on the NIH Chest X-ray dataset to restore noisy images. A hybrid loss function combining Mean Squared Error (MSE) and Structural Similarity Index (SSIM) enables the model to preserve fine anatomical structures while effectively suppressing noise, resulting in clearer and more reliable medical images.

Research Contribution

This work demonstrates the effectiveness of deep convolutional neural networks for medical image denoising. By combining DnCNN with a hybrid MSE-SSIM loss function, the proposed approach successfully enhances low-dose chest X-rays, reduces imaging noise, and preserves clinically important structures, making it a promising solution for AI-assisted medical imaging applications.
